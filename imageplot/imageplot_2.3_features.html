The 80 features ImagePlot 2 extracts from each image
Basic = the 6 brightness/saturation/hue statistics.
Standard = 21 named, individually interpretable features (the seven groups below).
All = 80, adding the technical groups (spatial grid + histograms) used mainly by PCA / t-SNE / UMAP.
Every value is computed on the image downscaled to 256 px.
Color & Tone — 9
FeatureWhat it measures
brightness_median
0–1		Overall lightness — the middle brightness across all pixels. 0 = very dark, 1 = very light.
brightness_stdev
0–1		Tonal contrast — how much brightness varies. High = strong dark-to-light contrast (chiaroscuro); low = flat, even lighting.
saturation_median
0–1		Overall colour intensity. 0 = grey / neutral, 1 = pure vivid colour.
saturation_stdev
0–1		Variation in colour intensity — a mix of vivid and muted areas (high) vs uniformly saturated or uniformly dull (low).
hue_median
0–360°		The prevailing hue on the colour wheel (≈0/360 red, 120 green, 240 blue). The dominant colour family.
hue_stdev
degrees		Spread of hues. Low = one colour family dominates (near-monochrome); high = many different hues present.
red_mean
0–1		Average red across the image.
green_mean
0–1		Average green across the image.
blue_mean
0–1		Average blue. The three channel means together describe the overall colour cast (e.g. high red + low blue = warm).
Tone & Hue Diversity — 2
FeatureWhat it measures
brightness_entropy
0–~5 bits		How many distinct brightness levels are present, regardless of where they sit. High = a rich tonal range (gradients, photographs); low = only a few tones (flat or two-tone graphics). Measures variety, not contrast or placement.
hue_entropy
0–~5 bits		Diversity of hues present. High = many colours; low = a limited palette.
Texture — 2
FeatureWhat it measures
local_contrast
0–1		Average brightness difference between neighbouring pixels — fine-grain texture / crispness. High = sharp detail or noise; low = smooth, soft.
edge_density
0–1		Fraction of pixels lying on a strong edge — overall busyness. High = many contours / detail; low = large smooth areas.
Orientation — 4
FeatureWhat it measures
pct_horizontal
0–1		Share of edges running roughly horizontally (horizons, bands, reclining forms).
pct_vertical
0–1		Share of edges running roughly vertically (uprights, columns, standing figures).
pct_diagonal
0–1		Share of edges at diagonal angles (gestural, dynamic, unstable compositions). The three percentages together sum to ≈1.
dominant_angle
0–180°		The single most common edge direction in the image.
Composition — 3
FeatureWhat it measures
symmetry
0–1		Mirror balance — how well one half mirrors the other (left↔right or top↔bottom), measured on the edge map so a blank background can't inflate it; reports the more symmetric of the two axes. High = centred / mirror-balanced; low = off-centre or non-mirror. Bilateral only — not rotational or diagonal symmetry.
dominance
0–1		Focused vs even — how concentrated the visual energy (edges) is across the frame. High = one area carries the weight (a single dominant figure); low = energy spread evenly (an all-over composition).
weight_offset
0–1		How far the centre-of-mass of visual energy sits from the frame centre. 0 = balanced; high = weight pushed toward a side or corner. Differs from symmetry: it measures placement of weight, not mirroring.
Format — 1
FeatureWhat it measures
aspect_ratio
w ÷ h		Shape of the image: <1 portrait, =1 square, >1 landscape.
Technical groups — 59  (All tier only)
FeatureWhat it measures
Spatial grid (27)
grid_*		Brightness, saturation and hue measured separately in each cell of a 3×3 grid (grid_TL_brightness … grid_BR_hue). Encodes where light and colour sit — the raw spatial layout behind composition. Not individually interpretable, but powerful for clustering.
Grayscale histogram (16)
gray_hist_00–15		Fraction of pixels in each of 16 brightness bins, dark → light — the full tonal distribution.
Saturation histogram (16)
sat_hist_00–15		Fraction of pixels in each of 16 colour-intensity bins, grey → vivid — the full saturation distribution.
In the app, the Standard features appear in the X / Y and Color-by menus grouped under these same headings, each with a one-word reminder. The technical groups appear only when you extract All (80).
